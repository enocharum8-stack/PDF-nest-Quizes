Evolution of Nigeria as a Political Unit

Nigeria was formed through gradual amalgamation: 1900 (Niger Coast Protectorate merged with Royal Niger Company territory), 1906 (Lagos Colony merged with Southern Protectorate), and 1914 (Northern + Southern Protectorates united by Lord Frederick Lugard on 1 January). The name "Nigeria" was coined by journalist Flora Shaw in 1897, later Lugard's wife. Reasons for amalgamation: cheap source of raw materials, ready market for British goods, manpower reserve, and reduced administrative cost since the South was richer than the North.

Formation of Political Parties

NNDP (1923) — first party, founded by Herbert Macaulay. NYM (1934/1936) — first party with national outlook. NCNC (1944) — founded by Macaulay & Azikiwe. Action Group (1951) — Awolowo, dominant in the West. NPC (1949) — Ahmadu Bello, dominant in the North. NEPU — Aminu Kano's leftist offshoot of NPC. Later republics: NPN, UPN, NPP (2nd Republic); SDP, NRC (3rd Republic); PDP, APC, APGA (4th Republic).

Colonial Rule & Administration

Britain colonised Nigeria for economic exploitation, raw materials, and trade monopoly, justified by the "civilizing mission." Key conquests: Fall of Benin (1897), Anglo-Aro War (1901-1902), Sokoto Caliphate conquest (1903). The British used Indirect Rule (introduced by Lugard) — successful in the North due to the centralised Sokoto Caliphate structure, but largely a failure in the acephalous Igbo South, leading to corrupt "Warrant Chiefs" and the 1929 Aba Women's Riot.

Nigerian Culture & Ethnic Groups

Nigeria has over 250 ethnic groups; the 3 majority groups are Hausa, Yoruba, Igbo. Notable minority groups: Kanuri, Fulani, Tiv, Nupe, Igala, Idoma, Jukun, Igbira, Birom. Igbo political system is acephalous/gerontocratic (no central king); Yoruba is monarchical (Oba); Hausa/Fulani is the Emirate system (Sarki/Emir) after the 1804 Sokoto Jihad led by Usman Dan Fodio. Material culture = tangible (art, tools, buildings); non-material = abstract (religion, language, values).

Trade & Economics of Self-Reliance

Indigenous trade relied on barter systems, village markets, and interregional trade routes, often tied to cultural festivals. Economics of self-reliance emphasizes local production and reduced dependence on external aid. The indigenous apprenticeship system (blacksmithing, weaving, pottery, leatherwork, wood carving) was informal, hands-on, master-apprentice based, and central to skills acquisition.

Reorientation Strategies & National Values

Key historical programmes: Operation Feed the Nation (1976, Gen. Obasanjo era per general history, though some notes cite 1983/Buhari), Green Revolution (1973, Gowon), Austerity Measures (1981, Shagari), WAI — War Against Indiscipline (1984, Buhari), WAIC — War Against Indiscipline and Corruption (1984, Buhari), MAMSER (1987, Babangida), and the National Orientation Agency (NOA) (1993, Abacha). The "3R's" framework = Reconstruction, Rehabilitation, Reconciliation.

Citizenship & Civic Responsibilities

Citizenship is acquired via jus soli (birth), jus sanguinis (parentage/blood), jure matrimonii (marriage), or naturalization. Nigerian fundamental rights are in Chapter IV of the 1999 Constitution (sections 24-43), including right to life, fair hearing, freedom of expression, and freedom from discrimination.
