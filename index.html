Week 1 — Simple Harmonic Motion (SHM)

SHM is motion where acceleration ∝ –displacement. Key equations: x = A sin(ωt), v = ω√(A²–x²), a = –ω²x. For a spring: T = 2π√(m/k). For a pendulum: T = 2π√(L/g). Total energy = ½kA² (constant). At equilibrium KE is max, at amplitude PE is max.

Weeks 2–3 — Waves & Wave Motion

A wave transfers energy without transferring matter. Key quantities: v = fλ, T = 1/f, ω = 2πf. Wave equation: y = A sin(ωt – kx). Transverse waves vibrate ⊥ to propagation (light, strings). Longitudinal waves vibrate ∥ to propagation (sound). Properties: reflection, refraction, diffraction, interference, polarisation (transverse only).

Weeks 4–5 — Sound Waves & Intensity

Sound is a longitudinal mechanical wave. Speed in air ≈ 330 m/s at 0°C, increases by 0.6 m/s per °C. Intensity = P/(4πr²). Sound level: β = 10 log(I/I₀) dB, where I₀ = 10⁻¹² W/m². Constructive interference: waves in phase → amplitude doubles. Destructive: 180° out of phase → cancellation.

Weeks 6–7 — Doppler Effect

Observed frequency changes when source/observer moves. General formula: f' = f(v ± v_o)/(v ∓ v_s). Use + in numerator when observer moves toward source; – in denominator when source moves toward observer. Applications: radar speed guns, medical ultrasound, astronomy (redshift).

Weeks 8–9 — Strings, Pipes & Harmonics

Standing waves on strings: f_n = nv/(2L). Closed pipe: f_n = (2n–1)v/(4L) (odd harmonics only). Open pipe: f_n = nv/(2L). Beats: f_beat = |f₁ – f₂|. Sonometer law: f ∝ √T, f ∝ 1/L, f ∝ 1/√μ.

Weeks 10–11 — Optics & Huygens' Principle

Mirror formula: 1/f = 1/u + 1/v, magnification m = –v/u. Snell's law: n₁ sin θ₁ = n₂ sin θ₂. Refractive index: n = c/v. Total internal reflection occurs when θ_i > θ_c (denser → less dense). Lens formula: 1/f = 1/v – 1/u. Power of lens: P = 1/f (in dioptres). Huygens' principle: every wavefront point is a source of secondary wavelets.
